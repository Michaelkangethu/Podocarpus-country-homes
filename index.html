About Us

Podocarpus cottages are the perfect destination for anyone who wants to escape the hustle and bustle of the city and enjoy a serene and relaxing vacation in the heart of nature. These cottages are situated on the eastern side of Mt Kenya, the second highest mountain in Africa and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The cottages are surrounded by lush greenery and offer a panoramic view of the mountain and the forest. You can wake up to the sound of birds singing and the sight of monkeys playing in the trees. You can also admire the spectacular sunrise and sunset over the mountain from your cottage window or balcony. The cottages are spacious and well-equipped with modern amenities, such as Wi-Fi, TV, hot water, and kitchen facilities. You can also enjoy delicious meals prepared with fresh ingredients from the local farms and markets. The staff are friendly and helpful, and will make you feel at home. You can also interact with other guests and share your experiences and stories. One of the main attractions of Podocarpus cottages is their proximity to Mt Kenya National park, which is only 200 metres away. The park covers an area of over 700 square kilometres and hosts a variety of flora and fauna, including elephants, buffaloes, leopards, zebras, antelopes, hyenas, and many more. The park also has several hiking trails that cater to different levels of difficulty and duration. You can choose to take a half day hike to the forest zone, where you can see exotic plants, waterfalls, and caves. Or you can opt for a full day hike to the alpine zone, where you can see glaciers, lakes, and rocky peaks. If you are feeling adventurous, you can also join a guided expedition to climb to the summit of Mt Kenya, which stands at 5,199 metres above sea level. The climb takes about four to six days, depending on the route and the weather conditions. It is a challenging but rewarding experience that will give you a sense of achievement and awe. Podocarpus cottages are also an ideal base for exploring the surrounding animal conservancies, such as Ol Pajeta, which is about 40 kilometres away. Ol Pajeta is one of the largest and most successful wildlife sanctuaries in Kenya, and home to the last two northern white rhinos in the world. You can also see other endangered species, such as black rhinos, Grevy's zebras, and chimpanzees. You can take a game drive or a guided walk through the conservancy and learn more about the conservation efforts and challenges. You can also visit the Sweetwaters Chimpanzee Sanctuary, which provides a safe haven for rescued chimpanzees from across Africa. Another place worth visiting is the Maumau caves, which are about 20 kilometres away from Podocarpus cottages. These caves are of historical significance, as they were used by the Maumau fighters during their struggle for independence from British colonial rule in the 1950s. The caves have been preserved as a national monument and a museum, where you can see artifacts, photos, and documents that tell th e story of the Maumau movement and its heroes. If you are a bird lover, you will be amazed by the diversity of birds that inhabit the area around Podocarpus cottages. There are over 300 species of birds that have been recorded in Mt Kenya National park alone, ranging from tiny sunbirds to majestic eagles. You can also see rare and endemic birds, such as the Jackson's f rancolin, the Abbott's starling, and the Sharpe's longclaw. You can take a birdwatching tour with an expert guide who will help you identify and appreciate these feathered beauties. Podocarpus cottages are more than just a place to stay, they are your home away from home. They offer you a unique opportunity to experience nature at its best, as well as learn about the culture and history of Kenya. Whether you are looking for adventure or relaxation, you will find it at Podocarpus cottages.

Our Services

If you are looking for a peaceful and scenic getaway in the heart of Kenya, l ook no further than Podocarpus cottages. These cozy and comfortable cottages are located on the eastern slopes of Mt Kenya, the second highest mountain in Africa. Podocarpus cottages are surrounded by lush greenery and stunning views of the mountain and the valley. You will feel like you are in a different world, away from the hustle and bustle of the city. Podocarpus cottages are not only a place to relax and unwind, but also a perfect base for exploring the rich natural and cultural heritage of the region. You can enjoy a variety of activities, such as:

Hiking

You can choose to hike in the nearby Mt Kenya National Park, which is only 200 metres away from the cottages. The park offers trails for different levels of fitness and experience, from half-day forest walks to full-day mountain treks. You will encounter diverse flora and fauna, including rare and endangered species like the giant forest hog, the bongo antelope, and the black rhino. You might even catch a glimpse of the snow-capped peak of Mt Kenya on a clear day.

Wildlife viewing

You can also visit some of the nearby animal conservancies, such as Ol Pejeta, which is home to the largest population of black rhinos in East Africa, as well as lions, elephants, giraffes, zebras, and more. You can also see the last two remaining northern white rhinos in the world, Najin and Fatu, who are under 24-hour protection at Ol Pejeta. You can also learn about the conservation efforts and challenges faced by these majestic animals

History

You can also learn about the history of Kenya by visiting the Mau Mau caves, which are located within Mt Kenya National Park. These caves were used by the Mau Mau fighters, who waged a guerrilla war against the British colonial rule in the 1950s. You can see the graffiti and carvings left by the rebels on the walls of the caves, as well as some of their weapons and belongings. You can also hear stories and legends about their bravery and sacrifice from the local guides.

Birdwatching

If you are a bird lover, you will be delighted by the variety and abundance of birds that you can see around Podocarpus cottages. The area is a birdwatcher's paradise, with over 200 species of birds recorded, including some rare and endemic ones like the Jackson's francolin, the Sharpe's longclaw, and the Aberdare cisticola. You can also spot some colourful and exotic birds like the scarlet-tufted malachite sunbird, the golden-winged sunbird, and the purple-throated cuckoo-shrike.
